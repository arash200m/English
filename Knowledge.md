#### Autophagy

Autophagy ("self-eating") is a natural cellular detoxification and recycling process that occurs during fasting, where the body breaks down damaged cells and proteins to renew itself. Fasting is the most effective way to trigger this process, as it lowers insulin levels and forces cells to find energy by cleaning up waste material.

##### How Long to Fast for Autophagy
- **Initial Stages:** Autophagy begins to increase around 12-16 hours of fasting.
- **Significant Activation:** Substantial autophagy is generally thought to occur between 16 and 24 hours of fasting.
- **Peak Benefits:** The highest levels of autophagy are thought to occur during extended fasts, peaking around 48 to 72 hours. 
BodySpec

##### Key Aspects of Autophagy Fasting
- **What it does:** It acts as a cellular "housekeeping" mechanism, clearing out old or broken organelles (like mitochondria) to protect against disease and promote longevity.
- **How to do it:** While intermittent fasting (e.g., 16:8) provides benefits over time, deeper, longer fasts (24+ hours) are required to reach peak autophagy.
- **What breaks it:** Eating or drinking anything that raises insulin (calories) will stop or slow the process. **Water, black coffee, and tea are generally allowed during the fast.**
- **Other Stimulants:** While fasting is most effective, a keto diet (high fat, low carb) or high-intensity exercise can also stimulate autophagy. 
Healthline

##### Important Considerations
- **Safety**: Always consult a healthcare provider before starting long-term fasting, as it may not be suitable for everyone.
- **After Fasting:** Breaking a fast with heavy or high-sugar foods can disrupt the benefits. 
Cleveland Clinic
